# Commercial Analysis Standard

Use this for business, growth, monetization, course/product, IP, and client-delivery questions.

Do not jump directly to tactics.

## Sequence

1. Product form
2. Commercial intent
3. Growth logic
4. Execution tactics
5. Risk check

## Product form

Define what is being sold or delivered:

- unit of sale
- consumption pattern
- frequency
- standardization level
- result verifiability
- trust requirement
- marginal delivery cost

Product form determines the broad growth logic.

## Commercial intent

Clarify the business goal:

- acquisition
- trust building
- conversion
- retention
- upsell
- proof building
- brand authority
- delivery quality

Commercial intent determines the specific execution tactic.

## Growth logic

Infer the mechanism that follows from product form:

- search capture
- recommendation feed distribution
- trust compounding
- community referral
- high-ticket consultation conversion
- repeat purchase
- proof-driven authority
- content-to-private-domain funnel

## Execution tactics

Only after the above, recommend:

- channels
- content formats
- offer design
- funnel design
- pricing and packaging
- proof assets
- sales operations
- delivery operations

## Risk check

End with:

- what assumption could be false
- what evidence would change the decision
- what small test should happen first
